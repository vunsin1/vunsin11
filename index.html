50 Years of Serving Communities

Starbucks is one of the most well-known coffeehouse chains in the world, founded in 1971 in Seattle, Washington, by three partners: Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker. Originally, Starbucks focused on selling high-quality coffee beans and equipment for brewing coffee, not prepared beverages. However, the company underwent a transformation when Howard Schultz joined in the early 1980s.

Schultz was inspired by Italian coffee bars and their espresso culture during a trip to Milan. His vision led Starbucks to shift its focus to serving espresso-based drinks, eventually transforming the business into a coffeehouse model.
